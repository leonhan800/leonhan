portable handwashing solution

Birds Eye Exploded View of Tray

Our design addresses the need for people living with motor function impairments, such as cerebral palsy, to wash their hands unassisted at home. Due to limited arm flexibility and hand strength, as well as the ineffectiveness of current solutions, it can be difficult for clients to apply, lather, and rinse soap. To address this, our tray is designed to fit onto the client's wheelchair and has a curved surface with a water exit hole that allows for easy and effective rinsing. Our solution also includes an automatic Arduino faucet, which delivers water without the need to physically flick up a faucet, and an automatic soap dispenser located on the water-resistant tray. The tray also includes loofahs and other soft materials, which allow the client to properly lather their hands with soap for cleanliness. Here are the steps that the client will follow to properly wash their hands: 1. Firstly, they will use an external automatic soap dispenser to apply soap onto their hands. 2. They will then rub their hands horizontally on the loofahs to work up a rich lather with the soap. 3. Next, they will hover their hand over the ultrasonic sensor to activate the water pump, which will dispense a refreshing stream of water onto their hands. 4. Lastly, they will place their hands under the tube that is connected to the water pump to rinse and lather their hands with water, ensuring that all the soap has been washed away.

General Design Overview

Arduino Circuit - Aayush Bhanot

The circuit function works by utilizing an ultrasonic sensor to detect the presence or absence of an object in its proximity. The ultrasonic sensor emits high-frequency sound waves, which bounce off the object and return to the sensor. Once the sensor has detected the object, it sends a signal to a relay module. The relay module acts as a switch, allowing the sensor to control the pump. When the sensor sends the signal to the relay module, the module activates a circuit that triggers the pump to turn on. The pump then allows water to travel through the pipe. The pump and sensor work together to allow the client access to water without the need to flick up a faucet
